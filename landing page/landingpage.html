The Invisible Weight  

of Cognitive Labor

How can we better recognize and value the invisible work it takes to manage a home? This project is an inquiry into how we might make the invisible dimensions of household work visible and tangible for people in their homes.
Cognitive labor  in the context  of the home is the mental work it takes to run a household.
It can feel like a never-ending mental checklist.”
Did we buy the pasta for dinner tonight?
I need to add apples to the grocery list.
I can’t forget to schedule my annual check up.”
The parking ticket is due soon. I have to get online and pay the bill tomorrow.
David’s birthday is this Saturday. Can we order  him a gift in time?”
My bike is at the shop. I need to coordinate when I can use the car to go pick it up.
These are the non-physical tasks that are mentioned in passing and imprecisely defined.
